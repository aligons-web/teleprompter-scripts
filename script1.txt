That’s how you move from being overwhelmed by problems to owning solutions.

Section 3: Overteaching vs Overdelivering
Omar made a powerful distinction:
•	Many new teachers and business owners overteach
•	But there is a difference between overteaching and overdelivering
•	Overteaching = dumping too much information, overwhelming people, leaving them confused
•	Overdelivering = providing so much clarity, value, and support that people feel empowered and ready to move
If I teach in a way that:
•	Makes it easy to understand
•	Helps people see themselves doing it
•	Clearly shows the benefit of moving forward
…then it becomes much easier for them to say “yes” to working with me.
So our job is not just to give a lot of information;
our job is to communicate in a way that converts insight into action.

Section 5: What to Teach vs How to Teach
Another key insight from Omar:
Don’t focus so much on teaching “how.”
Focus on teaching what, when, where, and why.
That means:
•	What to do and what not to do
•	When to do it and when not to do it
•	Where it makes sense and where it doesn’t
•	Why to do it and why it matters
When you go too deep into how during free or introductory teaching, people often:
•	Get lost in the details
•	Feel overwhelmed
•	Don’t take action anyway
But when you clearly explain the what, when, where, and why, they:
•	Gain clarity
•	Recognize the value of your guidance
•	Are more willing to step into your paid process to learn the detailed how

Section 6: Creating Your Own Intellectual Property (IP)
Omar also suggested using tools like:
•	Acronyms
•	Alliteration
•	Alphabet frameworks (A to Z types of models)
This helps you create memorable, branded processes that become your intellectual property.
Example using the letter P:
I’m a purpose-driven person providing perspective.
You could build a simple 4-step framework:
1.	Purpose – First, understand your purpose.
2.	Perspective – Second, get the right perspective on where you are and where you’re going.
3.	Pursuit / Progress – Third, pursue your goals and make consistent progress.
4.	Promise / Payoff – Fourth, experience the promise or reward that comes through the process.
People feel smart and supported when you:
•	Give them steps
•	Offer clear frameworks
•	Use acronyms or structured processes
This becomes your unique IP, your signature way of helping people.
But remember:
•	Don’t get over-technical
•	Keep it practical, relatable, and understandable
•	Always tie it back to their pain points and desired outcomes
When people feel:
•	Seen in their struggle
•	Understood in their environment
•	Guided toward where they want to go
…they trust you to help them navigate and elevate to the next level.

Section 7: The Power of Single-Minded Focus
One major point I want to emphasize in the next class is this:
Many people are stuck not because they lack talent or ideas, but because they are:
•	Too busy
•	Too divided
•	Too distracted
•	Doing too many things at once
Omar mentioned how he focused on just one core idea—teaching and selling—and how powerful that single focus became.
I want you to really hear this:
If you don’t pull back from all the things that divide your time, attention, and energy, you will not reach the place you truly desire.
Study highly successful people and you’ll find:
•	They did not become successful with a constantly divided mind.
•	They were not trying to do everything at once.
•	They committed to one main thing long enough to see it grow.
Even within one project or one goal, there is:
•	Plenty to build
•	Plenty to refine
•	Plenty to manage
You don’t need 10 projects; you need one clearly defined focus, and a commitment to see it through.
If you get nothing else out of this session, get this:
Your future success is directly linked to your ability to focus on one main thing and push it forward with consistency.
